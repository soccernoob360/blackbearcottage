The Cottage

Old bones, modern comfort — designed for slow weekends and long stays

Originally built in 1902 and gut-renovated in 2020, Black Bear Cottage blends its history with everything you actually want: a chef's kitchen, a wood-burning stove with a custom mosaic hearth, and the kind of quiet you came here for.

A kitchen you'll actually cook in

Stainless appliances, an eat-in breakfast bar, and enough counter space to make the dinner you've been dreaming about. Four bedrooms plus a bonus room sleep eight in the main house, with two updated bathrooms with walk-in showers.

When the meal's done, retreat to the library nook — stocked with books, games, and a guitar — or pull a chair up to the wood-burning stove.

Plus a tiny house out back

Just off the deck, a separate tiny house sleeps two in a cozy loft. Perfect for friends who want their own space, the grown-up kids, or anyone who's always wanted to wake up in their own little cabin in the woods.

Seasonal: the tiny house runs on space heaters in colder months and isn't plumbed — guests use the main house facilities.

The hot tub, open all year

At the edge of the deck, overlooking the forest — and open in every season. Soak under the stars on a summer night with the fireflies out, or sink into the steam in the middle of winter while snow settles on the hemlocks.

It's the thing guests mention most. One review put it simply: the hot tub while watching the snow melt outside.

The land is the whole point

Three private acres of meadow and forest, bordered by the Rail Trail — walk straight from the back door into a hike or a bike ride. The wraparound deck catches the sun all day: dining table and grill in the middle, fire pit at the edge of the trees, and a sledding hill when the snow comes.

Roast something over the fire. Watch the deer wander the backyard from the deck. This is the part you'll remember.

The Area

Two miles from Livingston Manor. A world away from everything else.

Livingston Manor has quietly become one of the most talked-about small towns in the Catskills — and Black Bear Cottage is five minutes from all of it.

Eat & Drink Main Street, five minutes away

Wood-fired pizza at The Kaatskeller, groceries and sandwiches at Main Street Farm, natural wine at Upstream — plus Catskill Brewery and Upward Brewing minutes from the door.

On the Water The birthplace of American fly-fishing

The Willowemoc runs just outside the village, and the Beaverkill is across the Manor. Some of the country's most storied trout water is at your fingertips.

On the Trail Hike from the back door

The Rail Trail starts behind the property. Beyond it: miles of state forest, golf at Sullivan County Country Club, horseback riding at Bridle Hill Farm, and alpacas at Buck Brook Farm.

Good to Know

Questions, answered

When are check-in and checkout?

Check-in is anytime after 3:00 PM with easy self check-in via lockbox. Checkout is by 11:00 AM. Need flexibility? Just ask — Nelson responds fast.

How many people can stay?

The main house sleeps 8 across four bedrooms and a bonus room (3 queens + 2 twins), with 2 full bathrooms. In season, the tiny house sleeps 2 more in a cozy loft.

Is the tiny house available year-round?

The tiny house is seasonal — it runs on space heaters in colder months and isn't plumbed, so tiny house guests use the main house facilities. It's a favorite for morning yoga and kids who want their own cabin.

Can I bring my dog?

Yes — Black Bear Cottage is pet friendly, and with three fenced-by-forest acres and the Rail Trail out back, dogs tend to have as good a weekend as their humans.

How do I get there from NYC?

It's about two hours from the George Washington Bridge — an easy straight shot up Route 17 to Livingston Manor, then two miles to the cottage on a quiet dead-end road.
